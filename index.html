Accessing the City

The bewildering metropolis

Modern cities can be demanding for anyone to navigate, with a plethora of intertwining streets, numerous different buildings, a sea of vehicles, and a maze of sidewalks and crossings.

Cities and the disabled

Cities pose an even greater number of challenges to people with some form of disability.

Several issues within architectural and infrastructural designs cause people with disabilities to be less mobile than and more dependent on other people.

Planning for disabilities

While architects and urban planners will likely have to consider these issues for future projects, we as developers must ask ourselves: “Can we use technology to help our societies overcome these issues, and perhaps even rectify them?”

Breaking Barriers

The present and the future

While some apps exist to guide wheelchair users and blind people through cities, they must utilize the areas that are already accessible, largely avoiding many of the challenges. The goal of Urban Access is to give City Councils and relevant organizations, and perhaps private businesses, concrete information about where inaccessibility has occurred, and what specifically needs to be fixed. All leading to our ultimate vision of a 100% accessible city.

Communicate the issues

Urban Access provides its users with the ability and opportunity to report the obstacles they face on their ventures through the city. Using GPS and photographic technologies, in combination with short written or recorded descriptions, users are able to send accurate reports of a wide variety of encountered barriers.

Urban Access

Assistance from city to city

While the ultimate goal of Urban Access is to transform every obstacle in our cities, we strive to provide assistance on every step of the journey. We make sure that our users can request help in any event that they cannot, for example enter a building, ascend a flight of stairs or access public transportation. The availability of the service depends on an active partnership within the city, and therefore is not currently available in every location.

Empowering the citizen

The main functionality of the app is available for free, as it’s designed to be a tool of empowerment to people with disabilities; allowing them to directly impact the cities they live in or visit. The paid service of requesting help puts users in direct contact with Urban Access’ local partners, whether they be public employees, organizations dealing with accessibility, local businesses or a sole proprietorship. These relations will vary from city to city, depending on local initiatives and projects.

The App Itself

Main Page

Simple and intuitive

A – Request on-the-spot assistance from our employees positioned around the capital. This is a paid service.
B – View your profile and how you present yourself to our employees.
C – Information about our company, our vision and our user terms.
D – Find out exactly where barriers in your city have been reported and where they have been fixed.
E – Report an accessibility barrier you encounter.

Report Barrier

Flexible and exact

A – Take a picture of the barrier you wish to report.
B – Overview over your position so you can be sure the correct location is being pinpointed.
C – Submit barrier.
D – See your previous submission as well as the progress that has been done towards fixing it.

Report Barrier

Flexible and exact

E – Send your submission after shortly describing it.

Request Help

Effective and responsive

A – Call or commence chatting with one of our local helpers, ready to get you through a barrier.
B – Submit your location with the push of a button, and one of our local helpers will come to assistance.
C – Check your position to make sure our helpers arrive at your exact location.

Profile

Clear and personal

A – View and change your profile picture, should you choose to have one.
B – Your full name.
C – Your age.
D – Which city you’re located in.
E – Your specified disability.
F – Read our privacy policy, and find out who can access your information.
G – Change or view your credit card information.

Overview

Visual and Systematic

A – Gray pins show locations with barriers that are under the process of being fixed.
B – Green pins show where barriers have been fixed the past month.
C – Red pins show where barriers have been submitted, but haven’t been looked in to yet.
